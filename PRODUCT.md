# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Broad and inclusive — all ages and genders, across Wellington. Primary context: someone on their phone searching for a quality local barber, deciding whether to book. Style-conscious but not vain; looking for a place that takes craft seriously without being exclusionary or intimidating.

## Product Purpose

Be Kind Barbers is a premium Wellington barbershop. The website has a dual mandate: establish brand credibility (so new visitors trust the quality before walking in) and convert that trust to bookings via the Timely widget. Success = someone landing on the site and booking an appointment, or leaving confident enough to walk in.

## Brand Personality

Craft · Warmth · Confidence

The "Be Kind" name is load-bearing — this isn't a cold, style-over-substance shop. It's exceptional at its trade and genuinely welcoming. The site should feel like the kind of barber who's as good at conversation as they are with scissors: assured but never intimidating, premium but never precious.

## Anti-references

- **Generic hair salon chains** (SuperCuts / Regis style): clinical, commodity, discount-feeling. No feature-grids, no before/after carousels, no coupon energy.
- **Overly hypebeast / streetwear barbershop**: heavy dark-trap aesthetic, aggressive attitude, exclusionary posturing. The brief is "kind" — the warmth must read.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth through material, not softness** — the dark palette and brass earn approachability through texture and light, not by going pale or pastel. The site feels like walking into a well-lit, wood-panelled room.
2. **The name does work** — "Be Kind" is a position. Copy and UX should reinforce inclusivity and ease: no jargon, no gatekeeping, frictionless booking.
3. **Mobile is the primary surface** — most customers arrive on a phone deciding whether to book. Every layout decision should prove itself at 390px before 1440px.
4. **Craft over flash** — one considered typographic choice beats three decorative flourishes. The site should feel like the work of someone who knows exactly what they're doing.
5. **Confidence without loudness** — no shouting. The hero heading can be large; the page should still feel composed, not salesy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA at minimum; push toward AAA on contrast where the dark palette allows it.
- Mobile-first layout and touch targets — booking on phone is the primary conversion path.
- Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations (the brand warmth doesn't depend on motion).
- Inclusive tone: copy and imagery should signal that everyone is welcome, not just a narrow demographic.
